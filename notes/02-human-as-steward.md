# 02. Human as Steward

“Human as gene” is a productive first intuition but an inaccurate literal map. A
gene is inherited information subject to selection; a human also interprets
context, makes value judgments, accepts responsibility, and can deliberately
change the rules.

In the ODD working model:

- purpose, values, constraints, policies, and retained knowledge resemble a
  genome;
- reusable rules and skills resemble genes;
- humans are stewards who author and revise that information and decide which
  changes may survive.

## Responsibilities that must not be delegated away

Humans remain accountable for:

1. defining whose health and whose interests matter;
2. setting boundaries that agents may not cross;
3. resolving value conflicts that metrics cannot settle;
4. authorizing irreversible or high-impact changes;
5. preserving an audit trail of why the system evolved;
6. stopping, pruning, or retiring unhealthy behavior.

## Engineering consequence

An ODD system needs explicit governance artifacts, not only prompts:

- a versioned system purpose;
- non-negotiable constraints;
- measurable but revisable health objectives;
- authority levels for different changes;
- provenance for observations, proposals, approvals, and outcomes.

The goal is not maximum autonomy. It is useful adaptation with legible human
accountability.

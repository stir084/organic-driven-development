# Patterns

This document will collect practices that put Organic-Driven Development into
action. A practice belongs here when it has a clear context, mechanism, tradeoff,
and observable outcome.

## Candidate patterns

### Viable Seed

Create the smallest end-to-end system that expresses the intended identity and
can receive feedback from its environment.

### Feedback Roots

Connect decisions to signals from users, runtime behavior, maintainers, and the
surrounding organization.

### Coherence Boundary

State the promises and invariants that must survive local change while allowing
implementation diversity inside the boundary.

### Healing Loop

Detect degradation, limit its impact, restore service, learn from the event, and
strengthen the system without relying on a single heroic intervention.

### Deliberate Pruning

Regularly remove structures whose cost exceeds their contribution, while
preserving knowledge needed to understand why they existed.

> These are initial hypotheses, not standardized patterns. They should earn their
> place through examples and critique.

# The Organic Software Manifesto

We are exploring how to cultivate software as a living system.

Through building, operating, repairing, and evolving software, we have come to
value:

- **Adaptation over prediction**
- **Continuous care over episodic maintenance**
- **Coherence over uniformity**
- **Resilience over the illusion of control**
- **Stewardship over ownership of a finished artifact**
- **Cooperation over isolated optimization**

The values on the right still matter. The values on the left guide us when the
two are in tension.

Software is not alive in a biological sense. The organic metaphor is useful
because it changes how we act: we observe before intervening, create conditions
for healthy growth, preserve useful diversity, remove harmful constraints, and
accept that responsible evolution has no final release.

Agents may perceive, decide, and act, but autonomy does not remove human
accountability. Humans remain responsible for the values encoded into the system,
the changes allowed to survive, and the consequences imposed on others.

Cooperation is not the absence of conflict. Healthy systems expose conflict,
contain harmful local behavior, and align specialized parts around the continued
health of the whole and its environment.
